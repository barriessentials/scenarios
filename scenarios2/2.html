That's correct. The Uninterrupted Scholars Act (USA) which amended FERPA allows state/local education agencies to release a child's education records to a state or local child welfare agency or tribal organization when the agency or tribal organization is "legally responsible for the child's care and protection."

The records may be specifically disclosed to an agency representative who has the right to access the child's case plan. Thus, under the USA the state and/or local education agency may disclose the requested information about an individual child who is the legal responsibility of the child welfare agency to the state child welfare agency.

The USA does not specifically address including such information in a child welfare agency database. Instead it contemplates that the disclosure would be made to an agency representative who is legally able to access the child’s case plan. However, if the child welfare agency database is structured such that only persons who have a right to access a child's case plan can access the child's education records, the disclosure is permissible under the USA.

Correct. The studies exception applies because it allows for the disclosure of PII from education records without consent to organizations conducting studies for, or on behalf of, schools, school districts, or postsecondary institutions.   • True
  • False

Correct. The child welfare agency may be able to apply the court order/subpoena exception to FERPA’s parental authorization rule because school officials may release education records in response to a court order or subpoena. In this case, the child welfare agency can request that the courts include in their individual dependency case orders language ordering the state and/or local education agency to release that child’s education records to the child welfare agency for research purposes.

Incorrect. The child welfare agency may be able to apply the court order/subpoena exception to FERPA’s parental authorization rule because school officials may release education records in response to a court order or subpoena. In this case, the child welfare agency can request that the courts include in their individual dependency case orders language ordering the state and/or local education agency to release that child’s education records to the child welfare agency for research purposes.

Usually, under FERPA, before releasing records in response to a court order, the school must make a "reasonable effort" to notify the parent.

However, under the USA amendment to FERPA, schools do not need to provide notice to parents prior to the release of records pursuant to the court order exception when the parents are parties to the child welfare case and, therefore, already are on notice that the school records will be shared.

In such cases, school can release education records without parental notification to any party listed on a child welfare case court order, such as the child welfare agency or caseworker, caretaker, children’s attorney, or court appointed special advocate.

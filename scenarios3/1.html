According to FERPA, the school district may release the information to Kathy if it takes any of the following steps:

  • The school district doesn't need to do anything before it may release the information.
  • The school district may release the information if it already notified parents about what directory information it will generally disclose.

Correct. FERPA defines a student's education record as records, files, documents, and other material maintained by a school or a person acting for the school and containing information directly related to a student. Reports from a school that include information about disciplinary actions are considered education records.

Incorrect. A school may release directory information without parental consent only if it already notified parents about what directory information it will generally disclose AND gave parents the option to notify the school that they do not want their child's directory information released.

Incorrect. A student under the age of 18 who is not enrolled in a post-secondary school cannot sign an authorization for the release of records. If the school did not comply with the notification requirements to release directory information, the school will need a signed authorization from the child's parent. A written authorization to release education records must:

  • Specify the records to be disclosed
  • State the purpose of the disclosure
  • Identify the party or class of parties to whom disclosure is to be made
  • Be signed and dated by the parent or guardian

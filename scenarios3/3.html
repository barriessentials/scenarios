Scenario 3

Law Enforcement Agency, School, Department of Behavioral Health, Social Services Agency

START

A school resource officer (SRO), who is employed by the school district's police department, is concerned about a student he has seen hanging out with known gang members and has seen out after curfew a number of times.

BACK NEXT

The SRO asks the guidance counselor if she can share with him the student's attendance and disciplinary records, grades, home address, and phone number.

He explains that he plans to go to the student's home and ask one or both of his parents to talk with him about how to help the student stay out of trouble.

BACK NEXT

The counselor knows that this request is governed by FERPA. She knows that there are special rules about "directory information."

Which information will be considered "directory information"? (check all that apply)

BACK NEXT

Partially correct.

Correct! The answer is address and phone number only. According to the U.S. Department of Education, directory information includes, but is not limited to, the following data about a student:

  • Name
  • Address and telephone number
  • Date and place of birth
  • Participation in school activities and sports
  • Dates of attendance

The counselor is aware that the school has met the requirements for releasing directory information without obtaining parental consent. This means the school has:

  • notified parents about the specific types of directory information it may disclose,
  • parents have the opportunity to notify the school in writing that they do not want information about their child disclosed.

BACK NEXT

The counselor shares the directory information with the SRO, but informs him that she cannot share the student's attendance and disciplinary records and grades without parental authorization.

However, there is an exception to the parental authorization requirement that may apply in this situation. FERPA allows disclosure without parental consent to:

  • Law enforcement officers pursuant to an interagency agreement.
  • Other school officials with a legitimate educational interest in the records.
  • Comply with a lawfully issued subpoena or judicial order.
  • Both B and C
  • All of the above
BACK NEXT

Incorrect. Interagency agreements do not supersede the consent requirements under FERPA.

Partially correct. The correct answer is D (Both B and C).

Correct!

Answer B is correct because FERPA allows disclosure without parental consent to other school officials who have a legitimate educational interest in the records. The school must include in an annual notification to parents the specific criteria for determining who is a school official and what constitutes a legitimate educational interest. Additionally, if the SRO (and any other individuals) in this case example were to receive information under this exception, they would have to be informed that they cannot re-release the information except in accordance with FERPA.

Answer C is correct because FERPA also allows disclosure without parental consent if required to comply with a court order or subpoena. If the school receives such an order or subpoena, it must make a "reasonable effort" to notify the parents about the court order/subpoena before releasing the records to law enforcement.

Incorrect. Schools providing drug and alcohol prevention and treatment services are bound by the Federal Drug & Alcohol regulations. FERPA does not apply to the SAP counselor.

Incorrect. In this case, Part 2 applies rather than HIPAA. Part 2 protects all information about any person who has applied for or been given diagnosis or treatment for alcohol or drug abuse at a federally assisted program. See 42 CFR §2.11 (definition of a "patient"). Although there are times when HIPAA and Part 2 apply to a record, this is not one of those times.

Correct. The Federal Drug & Alcohol (D&A) regulations at 42 CFR Part 2 apply to Jake's SAP records. In the substance abuse treatment field, confidentiality is governed by the federal drug and alcohol (FDAC) laws (42 U.S.C. §290dd-2) and regulations (42 CFR Part 2). These FDAC laws and regulations outline the limited circumstances when information about an individual's drug or alcohol treatment may be disclosed without the individual's consent. Here, Jake's records are clearly in the substance abuse field because that is what the SAP program does and what his caseworker wants information about.

Incorrect. The IDEA requires states to provide school-age children with "free, appropriate public education" in the "least restrictive environment." Part B of the act sets forth the process for providing such education, including evaluations for services, eligibility determinations, individualized education programs (IEPs), educational placements, and procedural safeguards. Part B also contains provisions regarding the confidentiality of these special education records. Jake's substance abuse records have nothing to do with any potential special education needs.

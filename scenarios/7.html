Scenario 7

Independent Living Provider and School

START

Seventeen year-old Charmaine is in 11th grade and is living in an Independent Living (IL) arrangement where she was placed by the dependency court. more

The IL provider case worker contacts you, Charmaine's high school guidance counselor, with some questions about Charmaine's educational progress.

BACK NEXT

Specifically, the IL worker asks you, the guidance counselor, the following information about Charmaine:

  • why she was not promoted to 12th grade at the start of the school year;
  • how many credits and what courses she needs to graduate;
  • her new anticipated graduation date;
  • her current course load; and
  • her grades thus far in this school year.
BACK NEXT

You presume that the IL worker wants the information to support Charmaine in her educational goals, but you don't know that for a fact.

You decide to research before you answer the IL worker's questions.

Through your research, you find that applies to the information because it governs the disclosure of educational records by a school counselor.

BACK NEXT

You wonder if the Uninterrupted Scholars Act amendment to FERPA applies to the IL caseworker in this case.

You write down some notes on FERPA:

FERPA as amended by USA allows "an agency caseworker or other representative of a State or local child welfare agency…who has the right to access a student's case plan" and is "legally responsible for the care and protection of a child" to obtain the child's education records.
BACK NEXT

You think that based on the plain language of the amendment, a private provider may count an "other representative" of a child welfare agency, so you search to see if the guidance explains further.

But, you realize that the guidance doesn't explain if the private provider (or contractor) can count as an agency representative for disclosure purposes. The only guidance provided covers redisclosure for private providers.

BACK NEXT

You write down some notes based on what you read in new U.S. Department of Education the Family Privacy Compliance Office guidance from May 2014, and highlight some relevant parts:

Child welfare agencies can only redisclose education records obtained through this exception to "an individual or entity engaged in addressing the student's education needs" who is authorized by the child welfare agency to receive the records, and consistent with other state confidentiality laws.

One example of an appropriate redisclosure would be to a contractor providing services to address a student's education needs.

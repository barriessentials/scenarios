Scenario 5

Special Education Teacher, Children and Youth Caseworker, and Foster Parent

START

Ten year-old Linus recently entered the child welfare system. more

The local children & youth agency places Linus with a foster family.

BACK NEXT

The children & youth case worker learned during his investigation that Linus has an Individualized Education Program (IEP).

The case worker and Linus's foster mother each contact the special education teacher at Linus's school to obtain a copy of Linus's IEP and tell the teacher that they want to be included in all future meetings at the school regarding Linus.

BACK NEXT

The case worker and the foster mother both attend an IEP meeting for Linus. At the end of the meeting, the special education teacher asks both the case worker and foster mother to sign the IEP on the line "parent/guardian". You are the caseworker, and you have a feeling that you can't sign the IEP. You try to think of the law that applies so you can formulate an answer for the teacher. You tell the teacher that the following law(s) apply:

  • FERPA
  • Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act (IDEIA)
  • FERPA and the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act (IDEIA)
BACK NEXT

Partially correct. FERPA applies because the foster parent and the case worker want Linus' school records. But, remember that the particular record they want is a special education record for which IDEIA applies. Therefore, IDEIA applies too.

Partially correct. The IDEIA applies, but so does FERPA. The IDEIA applies because we are talking about an IEP and although the IDEIA confidentiality regulations incorporate FERPA, they also contain some provisions that apply to only special education records. FERPA applies because the IEP may include some other elements that are in the education record more generally.

Correct. Both FERPA and the IDEIA apply because we are talking about an IEP and the education record more generally.

You explain to the teacher that under IDEIA a "parent" — the education decision maker in the special education system — can be:

  • a biological or adoptive parent,
  • a foster parent (if permitted under state law),
  • a guardian generally or specially authorized to make education decisions for the child,
  • someone with whom the child lives who acts as the parent,
  • someone who is legally responsible for the child's welfare, or
  • a surrogate parent.

You then explain to the teacher that under the IDEIA you, the caseworker, sign the IEP.

BACK NEXT

Therefore, if the foster mother is permitted under state law to act as the education decision-maker, and the birth or adoptive parent is not attempting to act as the education decision-maker, the foster mother can act as the education decision maker.

In that case, the school the IEP and other education records to her and invite her to IEP meetings.

The foster mother as educational decision-maker can also sign the IEP.

Note: If state law does NOT permit a foster parent to act as an IDEIA "parent," the juvenile court can appoint the foster parent as a "surrogate parent" for a child who is in the custody of a child welfare agency.

BACK NEXT

Then the teacher asks you if under FERPA she can share the IEP with you (the caseworker) and the foster mother. Fill in the blanks below with your advice.

Under a recent FERPA amendment, the Uninterrupted Scholars Act (USA), the children and youth caseworker access to Linus's education record and IEP.

Under the USA, the children and youth caseworker, who has the right to access a child's case plan under state law and whose agency has legal responsibility for the care of the youth, access the child's educational records.

BACK NEXT

The child welfare caseworker may then disclose the records to individuals/entities engaged in addressing the youth's education needs as long as the authorizes those individuals/entities to receive such records.

Thus, the educational information could possibly be shared with the foster mother for purposes of addressing the youth's education needs without consent of the parent if the child welfare agency authorizes it.

Great job! Keep reading to help the teacher understand more about releasing school records.

BACK NEXT

The teacher then asks you if there's any way to disclose the information directly to the foster mother without going through the child welfare agency. You answer the following:

The court in the child welfare agency may issue a/an authorizing the school to release records to the foster mother.

FERPA allows schools to release records in response to a court order. Normally, FERPA the school to notify the parent before disclosing records pursuant to a court order.

However, USA amended FERPA to specifically provide that if such an order is issued in a child welfare case to which the child's parent is a party, the school is not required to provide additional notice to the parent prior to releasing the records.
